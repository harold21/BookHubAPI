Privacy Policy

Effective date: February 14, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Sabbath School App ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, and protects your information when you use our mobile application.

1. Information We Collect

We may collect:

  • Account data: name, email address, user ID, role (student/instructor/admin), approval status.
  • Profile data: phone number, country code, document, gender, marital status, residence, nationality, occupation, class, birthdate, baptism date (optional), class role.
  • Attendance data: attendance date, class ID, answers to attendance questions, and optional "study contact" name/phone entries.
  • Technical data: basic app/device diagnostics needed for app reliability and security.

2. How We Use Information

  • Create and manage user accounts.
  • Provide attendance features and reports.
  • Allow instructors/admins to manage classes and students.
  • Support communication features (for example, opening WhatsApp links when available).
  • Maintain security, prevent abuse, and improve app performance.

3. Legal Basis (Argentina)

In Argentina, personal data is processed in accordance with Law No. 25,326 and related regulations, as applicable. Depending on the context, processing may be based on consent, performance of requested services, compliance with legal obligations, and legitimate interests compatible with applicable law.

4. Sharing of Information

We do not sell personal data. We may share data with service providers used to operate the app, such as:

  • Google Firebase (Authentication, Firestore, and related infrastructure).
  • Expo (app platform services, where applicable).

We may also disclose information if required by law.

5. Data Retention

We keep personal data only as long as necessary for app operation, legal obligations, and legitimate business needs. You may request deletion as described below.

6. Security

We apply reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect personal data. No method of storage or transmission is 100% secure.

7. Your Rights (Argentina)

Under Argentina’s Law No. 25,326, data subjects may request information, access, rectification, update, and suppression of personal data, as applicable.

Access requests are generally free of charge every six months. Controllers generally must respond to access requests within 10 calendar days, and to rectification/update/suppression requests within 5 business days, in accordance with applicable rules.

The control authority is the Agencia de Acceso a la Información Pública (AAIP). If your rights are not respected, you may file a complaint with AAIP.

9. International Transfers

Your data may be processed in countries other than your own through our service providers. We apply reasonable safeguards and contractual measures where required by applicable law.

10. Changes to This Policy
